# Belgium: compensation for electrician travel to different work sites

Date: 2026-05-07

## Situation

You live in Flanders and work as an electrician. You do not have one fixed workplace. The work site changes regularly across Flanders.

Previously, a colleague drove you to the work sites. This week, the planner/employer decided that you must travel with your own private car, without asking you first.

## Main conclusion

This should not be treated as a simple normal commute to one fixed workplace without checking the sector rules.

For electricians who go to different work sites, the relevant sector is likely:

**PC/CP 149.01 - elektriciens: installatie en distributie**

You should verify this on your payslip.

In this sector, there are specific rules for travel to work sites and mobility compensation.

## Important amounts found for PC 149.01 from 2026-02-01

The following amounts were found in current payroll/legal summaries for PC 149.01:

- `vergoeding eigen wagen naar werf`: **0.3450 EUR/km**
- `mobiliteitsvergoeding passagier`: **0.1732 EUR/km**
- `mobiliteitsvergoeding chauffeur zonder passagier`: **0.1817 EUR/km**
- `mobiliteitsvergoeding chauffeur met passagier`: **0.1929 EUR/km**

Meaning:

- If you drive your own car to the work site, the relevant amount may be **0.3450 EUR/km**.
- If you ride as a passenger with a colleague, the passenger mobility compensation may apply.
- If you drive colleagues, a driver mobility compensation may apply.

These amounts should be checked by the union against your exact status, payslip, contract, and applicable CBA/CAO.

## Why this matters

General Belgian law says that for a private car used for normal home-to-work travel, the employer is not always federally required to pay mileage compensation.

But your case is different because:

- you do not have one fixed workplace;
- you travel to changing work sites;
- you are likely in a sector with specific mobility rules;
- the employer changed the practical arrangement from riding with a colleague to using your own private car;
- if the contract does not require you to use your own car, the employer should not simply impose this without checking the rules.

## What to ask the union

Send the union this message:

```text
Ik werk als elektricien in Vlaanderen, zonder vaste werkplaats. Ik ga telkens naar verschillende werven. Vroeger reed ik mee met een collega, maar deze week heeft de planning beslist dat ik met mijn eigen wagen naar de werf moet rijden, zonder voorafgaand overleg.

Kunt u nakijken:
1. Onder welk paritair comité ik val, vermoedelijk PC 149.01?
2. Welke vergoeding ik moet krijgen voor verplaatsingen naar de werf met mijn eigen wagen?
3. Of de werkgever mij mag verplichten mijn privéwagen te gebruiken als dit niet uitdrukkelijk in mijn contract staat?
4. Of ik recht heb op vergoeding vanaf mijn woonplaats tot de werf en terug?
5. Welke bedragen vanaf 01/02/2026 van toepassing zijn: eigen wagen naar werf, mobiliteitsvergoeding passagier/chauffeur?
```

## What to ask the employer

Send the employer or planner this short written question:

```text
Kunt u schriftelijk bevestigen welke kilometervergoeding en mobiliteitsvergoeding wordt toegepast nu ik met mijn eigen wagen naar de werf moet rijden? Gelieve ook te bevestigen op basis van welke CAO of arbeidsreglement dit wordt opgelegd.
```

## Documents to collect

Keep these documents before contacting the union:

- payslips showing the paritair comité / commission paritaire;
- employment contract;
- work regulations / arbeidsreglement if available;
- weekly planning;
- addresses of the work sites;
- kilometers from home to each work site and back;
- messages from the planner saying you must use your own car;
- proof that previously you rode with a colleague, if available.

## Practical position

Do not frame this as only "my employer did not ask me nicely." Frame it as:

1. I have no fixed workplace.
2. I work on changing work sites.
3. Until now I was transported by a colleague.
4. Now I am required to use my private car.
5. I want written confirmation of the applicable CAO/CCT rule and the exact kilometer/mobility compensation.
