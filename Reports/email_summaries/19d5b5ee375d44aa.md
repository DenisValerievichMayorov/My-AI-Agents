Probiotics vs. enzymes

 

Probiotics vs. digestive enzymes — what’s the difference and when should you take each?

Our stomach and gut are much more than just a system for breaking down food; it is a complex environment where thousands of microorganisms and enzymes work together around the clock to support our health. Two supplements often mentioned in this context are probiotics and digestive enzymes, but what really distinguishes them?

What are probiotics?
Probiotics are live bacteria that resemble the beneficial bacteria naturally found in our gut flora. They are sometimes referred to as beneficial bacteria or lactic acid bacteria and contribute to normal gut function and help reduce gas/bloating.

Probiotics are beneficial when you want to:
• Support the gut flora (e.g. after a course of antibiotics).
• Reduce IBS-like symptoms.
• Support the immune system.

There are different types of probiotics depending on the bacterial strains and how many CFU (colony-forming units, i.e. live bacteria) they contain. Some are broader and more potent, while others are milder and more basic.

What are digestive enzymes?
Digestive enzymes are proteins that break down the food we eat, for example:
• Amylase for carbohydrates
• Lipase for fats
• Protease for protein

They help the body break down food more efficiently, which can reduce discomfort such as heaviness in the stomach, gas, and bloating.
